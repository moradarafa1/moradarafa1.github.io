How to Drink More Water Daily and Make It a Lasting Habit

Drinking enough water every day is one of the simplest habits you can build, yet most people fall short without realising it. Even mild dehydration affects concentration, energy, and mood long before thirst kicks in. The good news is that consistent hydration is a habit, and like any habit, it can be built deliberately and sustainably.

Why Do We Forget to Drink Water Even Though We Know We Should?

The obstacle is rarely willpower. Water has no taste or smell, and it is easily forgotten during a busy day. The first phase of Habit Reversal Training (HRT) is awareness: identifying exactly when you skip water and what patterns or situations come before that lapse. Once you see the pattern clearly, you can design a practical response.

How Much Water Do You Actually Need Each Day?

Most health authorities recommend between 2 and 2.5 litres per day for a typical adult in a moderate climate, with more needed in heat or during exercise. A simple self-check is urine colour: pale yellow means good hydration, while dark yellow signals a deficit. Thirst is a late alarm, so do not rely on it as your only cue to drink.

Building the Cue-Routine-Reward Loop for Water

In HRT, a new positive behaviour is anchored to an existing reliable trigger. Link water intake to fixed daily events: a glass right after waking up, one before each meal, and one at each prayer time. These natural triggers remove the need for conscious effort over time and turn hydration into an automatic part of your day.

Designing Your Environment to Make Drinking Easy

Place a visible water bottle on your desk or nightstand, because visual cues are among the strongest habit triggers. Keep a dedicated glass at your workstation and use the Awwad app to log your intake and maintain a daily streak, which turns a small action into a visible, satisfying accomplishment. When drinking water is effortless to start, the habit grows without relying on willpower.

Sustaining the Habit Beyond the First Two Weeks

Research suggests new habits take anywhere from 21 to 66 days to solidify, depending on the person and the behaviour. Track your daily intake, celebrate consistency rather than punishing slip-ups, and adjust your target upward on hot days or training days to stay accurate to your real needs. A streak you can see motivates you to protect it.

FAQ

Do juices and tea count toward my daily water intake?

Unsweetened herbal teas and green tea do contribute to hydration, but plain water remains the best option since it contains no calories or additives. Caffeinated drinks and sugary beverages can increase fluid loss, so they should not serve as your primary source of hydration.

What is the best time of day to drink water?

A glass of water immediately after waking helps the body resume normal functions after hours without fluid. A glass before each meal supports digestion and can reduce overeating. Spreading intake evenly throughout the day is more effective than drinking large amounts at once, since the body absorbs water gradually and efficiently.

How long does it take to build a consistent water-drinking habit?

Research shows habit formation takes anywhere from 21 to 66 days, depending on the individual and the specific behaviour. Pairing water with clear daily triggers and celebrating each successful week speeds up the process and makes the habit stick over the long term.
